Nature Scientific Reports 2025

Testing the Limits of Large Language Models in Debating Humans

J. Flamino, M.S. Modi, B.K. Szymanski, B. Cross & C. Mikolajczyk

LLM Agents Bayesian Regression Human Subject Study GPT-4 / Llama 2

Rigorously tested LLM-driven agents as debate partners and confederates in a pre-registered study with 251 participants across 37 games. Agents blended in and focused on debate topics better than humans, improving overall productivity.

However, humans perceived agents as less convincing — agents required 6.25× more conversations to induce equivalent opinion switches. Established behavioral signatures distinguishing human from AI-generated conversational data through Bayesian multilevel logistic regression.

Entropy (MDPI) 2025

Using LLMs to Infer Non-Binary COVID-19 Sentiments of Chinese Microbloggers

J.C. Hu, M.S. Modi & B.K. Szymanski

Llama 3 8B Few-Shot Prompting Sentiment Analysis Weibo Data

Analyzed over 4 million Weibo posts during the initial COVID-19 outbreak (Nov 2019 – Mar 2020). Used Meta Llama 3 8B with few-shot prompting to classify posts into four sentiment categories — positive, negative, sarcastic, and neutral — going beyond traditional binary classification.

Revealed how social events and public health measures shifted public sentiment over time, with sarcasm captured as a distinct dimension reflecting potential self-censorship on Chinese social media platforms.

IEEE Communications Magazine 2024

Dynamics of Ideological Biases of Social Media Users

M.S. Modi, J. Flamino & B.K. Szymanski

Network Analysis Community Detection NLP Flow Matrices

Tracked the evolution of user viewpoint groups on Twitter and Parler through large-scale social media data processing (60GB+). Demonstrated that homophily drives group formation — users seek groups whose members hold viewpoints sufficiently close to their own.

Found that Twitter evolved toward bimodal viewpoint distribution, while Parler formed a single dominant echo chamber. Proposed a framework using flow matrices and bias tracking to capture inter-group movement dynamics.

arXiv Preprint 2025

Analysis of Collaboration in CS Prizewinning with a Nobel-Turing Comparison

B.K. Szymanski, Y. Zhang, B. Uzzi & M.S. Modi

Science of Science Network Science CEM Matching LLM Classification

Analyzed over 100 scientific prizes and collaboration behaviors of 5,000+ CS prizewinners. Found that prizewinners collaborate earlier and more frequently with other prizewinners than non-awardees.

Using Coarsened Exact Matching (CEM) and regression, demonstrated that strength of collaboration increases the likelihood of winning awards. Examined the trend of CS researchers winning Nobel Prizes and used LLM APIs to classify award instances as single-field or interdisciplinary.

Active Development

MixUp — AI-Agentic Social Media Community

Shahid Modi — Lead Developer & Researcher

LLM Agents Knowledge Graphs RAG SFT + DPO Graph Database Python / Node.js

An AI-agentic social media platform that resolves limitations of existing simulated social environments by incorporating authentic community dynamics — influencers, communities, hubs, and natural communication patterns. MixUp allows the creation of agents with human-like personas derived from real social media user profiles, deployed in a Reddit-like structure that encourages community formation and strong relationships.

Technical Architecture

  • Agent Personas: Generated from real user profiles using dataset curation pipelines; each agent has a unique personality, communication style, and set of interests.
  • Alignment Pipeline: Supervised fine-tuning (SFT) on curated conversational data followed by Direct Preference Optimization (DPO) for behavioral alignment.
  • Emotion Mirroring: Agents adjust tone and emotional register in real time through knowledge graphs for Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG).
  • Graph Substrate: Underlying graph database structure where agents act as nodes — enabling simulations of public opinion formation, opinion change over time, and community evolution.
  • Research Bed: Designed as a research platform for running controlled simulations of social dynamics at arbitrary scale and complexity.
