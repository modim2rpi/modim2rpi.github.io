Nature Scientific Reports 2025

Testing the Limits of Large Language Models in Debating Humans

J. Flamino, M.S. Modi, B.K. Szymanski, B. Cross & C. Mikolajczyk

LLM Agents Bayesian Regression Human Subject Study GPT-4 / Llama 2

A pre-registered study rigorously testing LLM-driven agents as debate partners and confederates alongside 251 human participants across 37 opinion-consensus games.

  • Designed three game types — all-human (HH), all-agent (AA), and mixed (AH) — with six-player anonymous debate sessions on diet preferences, collecting 713 conversations and 15,804 messages.
  • Agents blended in convincingly and stayed more on-topic than humans, improving overall debate productivity across mixed games.
  • Bayesian multilevel logistic regression revealed agents need 6.25× more conversations to induce the same number of human opinion switches as human-to-human debates.
  • Humans perceived agents as significantly less confident — perceived confidence ratings were 1.8× lower for agents vs. other humans (95% credible interval).
  • Established distinct behavioral signatures between human and AI-generated conversational data, including differences in message cadence, opinion-switching frequency, and productivity distributions.

An LLM-Encoder Ensemble for Coarse-to-Fine Valence-Arousal Sentiment Prediction

M.S. Modi & B.K. Szymanski

Qwen3-8B PEFT + LoRA Encoder Distillation Multilingual NLP

Trained LLM-to-encoder ensemble models for multilingual valence-arousal sentiment inference, combining large language models with efficient encoder architectures.

  • Trained Qwen3-8B with PEFT + LoRA and distilled into efficient encoders for scalable inference on the shared task benchmarks.
  • Evaluated alternative solutions with rigorous ablation studies on tokenization, learning rates, and layer freezing schedules to understand each component's contribution.
  • Achieved top 6 RMSE overall among 15 multi-language entries with 1st place for an English subset.
  • Implemented multi-stage fine-tuning strategies — domain-adaptive pre-training followed by task-specific optimization to boost performance on out-of-domain data.
  • Designed ensemble methods combining predictions from multiple model variants to improve robustness and reduce variance across evaluation metrics.

Entropy (MDPI) 2025

Using LLMs to Infer Non-Binary COVID-19 Sentiments of Chinese Microbloggers

J.C. Hu, M.S. Modi & B.K. Szymanski

Llama 3 8B Few-Shot Prompting Sentiment Analysis Weibo Data

Used Meta's Llama 3-8B model for four-category sentiment analysis of Chinese social media Weibo, classifying ~1 million posts during the initial COVID-19 outbreak (Nov 2019 – Mar 2020).

  • Classified posts into four sentiment categories — positive, negative, sarcastic, and neutral — going beyond traditional binary classification using few-shot prompting on the Llama 3 8B Instruction-tuned model.
  • Validated results using a frontier LLM-as-a-judge and synthetically generated data, outperforming traditional lexicon-based and SVM approaches in nuance and generalizability.
  • Captured sarcasm as a distinct sentiment dimension, revealing its use as a potential self-censorship mechanism on Chinese social media to avoid community-driven moderation.
  • Demonstrated how social events and government public health measures caused measurable shifts in sentiment distributions — initial positive support gradually transitioned to increased negativity.
  • Presented a scalable LLM-based pipeline for non-binary Chinese text sentiment classification, outperforming traditional lexicon-based and SVM approaches in nuance and generalizability.

IEEE Communications Magazine 2024

Dynamics of Ideological Biases of Social Media Users

M.S. Modi, J. Flamino & B.K. Szymanski

Network Analysis Community Detection NLP Flow Matrices

Tracked the evolution of user viewpoint groups on Twitter and Parler through large-scale social media data processing (60GB+) during the 2020 U.S. Presidential election period.

  • Assigned initial and final political biases to users by computing the average bias of URL links they posted during their first and last months of activity (Sep–Dec 2020).
  • Demonstrated that homophily drives group formation online — users seek groups whose members hold viewpoints sufficiently close to their own, or they leave the platform.
  • Twitter evolved toward a bimodal viewpoint distribution with two stable poles (liberal and conservative), while Parler converged into a single dominant Right-leaning echo chamber.
  • Constructed flow matrices capturing inter-group user migration to quantify how individuals transition between ideological groups over the tracking period.
  • Extended prior offline homophily findings (campus student interactions) to platform-wide online settings, showing the same social dynamics govern group retention and polarization at scale.

arXiv Preprint 2025

Analysis of Collaboration in CS Prizewinning with a Nobel-Turing Comparison

B.K. Szymanski, Y. Zhang, B. Uzzi & M.S. Modi

Science of Science Network Science CEM Matching LLM Classification

Comprehensive analysis of over 100 scientific prizes and the collaboration behaviors of 5,000+ computer science prizewinners from 1965 to 2022.

  • Built collaboration networks from the SciSciNet dataset linking 125 recognized CS prizes to 5,416 scholars, enabling large-scale analysis of prizewinning dynamics.
  • Found that prizewinners collaborate earlier and more frequently with other prizewinners than researchers who have not yet received similar recognition.
  • Used Coarsened Exact Matching (CEM) and regression to demonstrate that strength of collaboration with prizewinners increases the likelihood of eventually winning an award.
  • Discovered that recipients of general CS prizes collaborate more than specialists, and that post-prizewinning collaborators are more likely to become future prizewinners.
  • Examined the trend of CS researchers winning Nobel Prizes and used LLM APIs to classify award instances as single-field or interdisciplinary, contrasting Nobel-Turing winner trajectories.

Active Development

MixUp — AI-Agentic Social Media Community

Shahid Modi — Lead Developer & Researcher

LLM Agents Knowledge Graphs RAG SFT + DPO Graph Database Python / Node.js

An AI-agentic social media platform that resolves limitations of existing simulated social environments by incorporating authentic community dynamics — influencers, communities, hubs, and natural communication patterns. MixUp allows the creation of agents with human-like personas derived from real social media user profiles, deployed in a Reddit-like structure that encourages community formation and strong relationships.

Technical Architecture

  • Agent Personas: Generated from real user profiles using dataset curation pipelines; each agent has a unique personality, communication style, and set of interests.
  • Alignment Pipeline: Supervised fine-tuning (SFT) on curated conversational data followed by Direct Preference Optimization (DPO) for behavioral alignment.
  • Emotion Mirroring: Agents adjust tone and emotional register in real time through knowledge graphs for Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG).
  • Graph Substrate: Underlying graph database structure where agents act as nodes — enabling simulations of public opinion formation, opinion change over time, and community evolution.
  • Research Bed: Designed as a research platform for running controlled simulations of social dynamics at arbitrary scale and complexity.
